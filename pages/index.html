I'm a Senior Researcher at the University of Oxford as part of the Torr Vision Group, and a Safe & Ethical AI Research Fellow at The Alan Turing Institute, the UK's national institute for data science.

Before this, I was a Postdoctoral Research Assistant in the Torr Vision Group, and a Postdoctoral Scholar in the Computation and Cognition Lab at the Department of Psychology at Stanford, working with Noah Goodman. Prior to that I obtained my PhD at the Center for the Computational Study of Cognition, Communication, Control, and Perception, at Purdue University, working with Jeff Siskind.

My research broadly involves the confluence of computer vision, robotics, natural-language processing, cognitive science, and elements of cognitive neuroscience, leading towards a central research goal to better understand perception and cognition with a view to enabling human-intelligible machine intelligence.
